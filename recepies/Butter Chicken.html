Butter Chicken


Butter Chicken, also known as Murgh Makhani, is a popular Indian dish made with tender chicken pieces cooked in a rich, creamy tomato-based sauce. It is known for its mild spiciness, buttery flavor, and aromatic spices, making it a favorite in Indian cuisine around the world.

Ingredients:

  • 500g boneless chicken (cut into bite-sized pieces)
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1 cup tomato puree
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 1 onion (finely chopped)
  • 2 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 tablespoon ginger (grated)
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh cilantro leaves (for garnish)
  • Cooked basmati rice or naan bread (for serving)

Instructions:

  1. Marinate the Chicken: In a bowl, combine the chicken pieces with half of the garam masala, turmeric powder, chili powder, and salt. Mix well and let it marinate for at least 30 minutes (or overnight in the refrigerator for better flavor).
  2. Cook the Chicken: In a large skillet or pan, heat 1 tablespoon of butter over medium heat. Add the marinated chicken pieces and cook until they are browned and cooked through. Remove the chicken from the pan and set it aside.
  3. Prepare the Sauce: In the same pan, add the remaining butter. Saut√© the chopped onions until they become translucent. Add the minced garlic and grated ginger, cooking for another minute until fragrant.
  4. Add Spices: Stir in the remaining garam masala, cumin powder, and a pinch of salt. Cook for a minute to toast the spices.
  5. Add Tomato Puree: Pour in the tomato puree and cook for about 10-15 minutes, allowing it to thicken and the flavors to meld together.
  6. Add Cream: Reduce the heat to low and stir in the heavy cream. Simmer for a few minutes until the sauce is creamy and well combined.
  7. Combine Chicken and Sauce: Return the cooked chicken pieces to the pan, stirring them into the sauce. Let it simmer for another 5-10 minutes to allow the chicken to absorb the flavors of the sauce.
  8. Garnish and Serve: Garnish with fresh cilantro leaves. Serve hot with cooked basmati rice or naan bread.
