Pad Thai


Pad Thai is a popular Thai street food dish made with stir-fried rice noodles, eggs, tofu or shrimp, and a flavorful sauce. It is typically garnished with crushed peanuts, lime wedges, and fresh herbs, creating a perfect balance of sweet, sour, salty, and spicy flavors.

Originating in Thailand, Pad Thai has become a beloved dish around the world for its unique combination of textures and tastes. The dish is quick to prepare and often cooked to order by street vendors. Each serving can be customized with extra chili, sugar, or lime to suit individual preferences. Whether enjoyed as a quick lunch or a hearty dinner, Pad Thai offers a delicious introduction to Thai cuisine.

Ingredients:

  • 200g rice noodles
  • 200g shrimp or tofu (cubed)
  • 2 eggs
  • 3 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 cup bean sprouts
  • 2 green onions (sliced)
  • 1/4 cup crushed peanuts
  • 1 lime (cut into wedges)
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon tamarind paste
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon chili flakes (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Noodles: Soak the rice noodles in warm water for about 20-30 minutes until they are soft. Drain and set aside.
  2. Make the Sauce: In a small bowl, mix together the fish sauce, soy sauce, tamarind paste, sugar, and chili flakes (if using). Set aside.
  3. Cook the Protein: Heat 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil in a large pan or wok over medium-high heat. Add the shrimp or tofu and cook until they are cooked through. Remove from the pan and set aside.
  4. Cook the Eggs: In the same pan, add a little more oil if needed. Crack the eggs into the pan and scramble them until they are just cooked. Push them to one side of the pan.
  5. Stir-Fry: Add the minced garlic to the pan and saut√© for about 30 seconds until fragrant. Add the drained rice noodles and pour the sauce over them. Toss everything together to coat the noodles evenly with the sauce.
  6. Add Vegetables: Add the cooked shrimp or tofu back into the pan along with the bean sprouts and sliced green onions. Stir-fry for another 2-3 minutes until everything is well combined and heated through.
  7. Serve: Transfer the Pad Thai to serving plates. Garnish with crushed peanuts and lime wedges on the side. Serve immediately.
